**Toronto Single-Day Travel Itinerary**

**Morning**

1. **8:00 AM - Breakfast at Lady Marmalade**
   - **Description:** Start your day with a hearty breakfast at this beloved east-end brunch spot known for its locally sourced ingredients and creative menu. Try their famous eggs benedict or the delectable breakfast poutine.
   - **Travel Method:** Walk from downtown (if staying nearby) or take the TTC and get off at Broadview Station, then a short 5-minute walk.
   - **Expected Cost:** $15–$25
   - **Notable Highlights:** Cozy atmosphere with great views of Riverdale Park.

2. **9:30 AM - Distillery District Exploration**
   - **Description:** Wander through this historic district known for its charming cobblestone streets and well-preserved Victorian-era industrial architecture. Explore art galleries, boutiques, and unique shops.
   - **Travel Method:** Take the TTC or a short 10-minute taxi from Lady Marmalade.
   - **Expected Cost:** Free (shopping and snacks extra)
   - **Notable Highlights:** Art installations and historical architecture.

**Late Morning**

3. **11:00 AM - St. Lawrence Market**
   - **Description:** Dive into a cultural hub of food and fresh produce, perfect for grabbing a light pre-lunch bite or local pastry. Sample some iconic Canadian peameal bacon sandwiches.
   - **Travel Method:** 15-minute walk from Distillery District or take the King Streetcar.
   - **Expected Cost:** $5–$15
   - **Notable Highlights:** Market named one of the world’s best food markets by National Geographic.

**Afternoon**

4. **12:30 PM - Lunch at Kensington Market**
   - **Description:** Explore this vibrant and eclectic neighborhood known for its multicultural food options. Choose from Jamaican jerk, Mexican tacos, or vegan delights.
   - **Travel Method:** Subway or a 15-minute streetcar ride to Spadina and Dundas.
   - **Expected Cost:** $20–$30
   - **Notable Highlights:** Diverse food options set amidst vintage shops and street art.

5. **2:00 PM - Royal Ontario Museum (ROM)**
   - **Description:** Visit Canada’s largest museum showcasing art, world culture, and natural history. Don’t miss the renowned dinosaur exhibits.
   - **Travel Method:** Walk or short subway ride from Kensington Market.
   - **Expected Cost:** $23 for adult entry
   - **Notable Highlights:** Architecturally notable “Crystal” building extension.

**Late Afternoon**

6. **4:30 PM - Jazz-Themed Vinyl Shop: Cosmos Records**
   - **Description:** Visit Cosmos Records, a haven for jazz and soul record enthusiasts. Browse through an impressive collection of vinyl records, perfect for collectors and music lovers alike.
   - **Travel Method:** 10-minute walk from the ROM.
   - **Expected Cost:** Prices vary depending on purchases.
   - **Notable Highlights:** Expert staff with great recommendations and rare finds.

**Evening**

7. **6:00 PM - Dinner at The Rex Hotel Jazz & Blues Bar**
   - **Description:** Enjoy a classic jazz bar experience with a range of live music performances. Relish the gastropub menu, including comfort foods and craft beers.
   - **Travel Method:** Short walk or streetcar ride on Queen Street.
   - **Expected Cost:** $25–$40 (including performance cover)
   - **Notable Highlights:** Premier destination for jazz fans, with live music multiple times per week.

**Night**

8. **8:30 PM - CN Tower Observation Deck**
   - **Description:** Conclude your day with spectacular nighttime views of the cityscape from one of Toronto’s most famous landmarks. Brave the glass floor for an adrenaline rush.
   - **Travel Method:** Short taxi ride or walk from The Rex.
   - **Expected Cost:** $43 for adult entry.
   - **Notable Highlights:** Panoramic 360-degree city views.

This itinerary offers a fulfilling blend of Toronto's rich cultural, culinary, and musical landscape, while being mindful of travel and time logistics. Enjoy your day in Toronto!
